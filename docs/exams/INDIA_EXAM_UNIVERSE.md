## Exam 1: NEET-UG

**Conducting body:** NTA
**Candidates:** 20–24 lakh annually
**Purpose:** Admission to MBBS, BDS, AYUSH across India

### Pattern
| Section | Questions | Marks | Negative Marking |
|---|---|---|---|
| Physics | 45 (35 attempt) | 140 | −1 per wrong |
| Chemistry | 45 (35 attempt) | 140 | −1 per wrong |
| Biology (Bot+Zoo) | 90 (70 attempt) | 280 | −1 per wrong |
| **Total** | **180 (140 attempt)** | **720** | |

**Duration:** 3 hours 20 minutes
**Type:** MCQ only (single correct)
**Medium:** 13 languages

### Syllabus
**Physics:** Class 11 + 12 NCERT — Mechanics, Thermodynamics, Waves, Optics, Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetism, Modern Physics, Semiconductor
**Chemistry:** Class 11 + 12 NCERT — Physical (Mole concept, Thermodynamics, Equilibrium, Kinetics, Electrochemistry), Organic (GOC, Reactions, Named reactions), Inorganic (p-block, d-block, coordination compounds)
**Biology:** Class 11 + 12 NCERT — Cell biology, Genetics, Evolution, Plant physiology, Human physiology, Reproduction, Ecology, Biotechnology

### Key Characteristics
- 99% of questions are directly from NCERT (line-by-line)
- Biology (280 marks) = 38% of total — dominant subject
- Speed matters: 140 questions in 200 minutes = 85 seconds per question
- Single correct MCQ: negative marking punishes guessing
- State quota vs All India quota splits

### Similar Exams (same syllabus)
- AIIMS Nursing Entrance (NCERT-based PCB)
- AFMC MBBS (NEET-equivalent + SSB)
- JIPMER (discontinued, merged with NEET)
- AYUSH NEET PG (post-MBBS, same framework)
- CUET Biology stream

## Exam 2: JEE Main

**Conducting body:** NTA
**Candidates:** 10–12 lakh annually
**Purpose:** Admission to NITs, IIITs, GFTIs; qualifier for JEE Advanced

### Pattern
| Section | Questions | Type | Marks |
|---|---|---|---|
| Physics | 20 MCQ + 5 Integer | Single correct + Numerical | 100 |
| Chemistry | 20 MCQ + 5 Integer | Single correct + Numerical | 100 |
| Mathematics | 20 MCQ + 5 Integer | Single correct + Numerical | 100 |
| **Total** | **75** | | **300** |

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Negative marking:** −1 for wrong MCQ, 0 for wrong integer
**Sessions:** January + April (best score counts)

### Syllabus
**Mathematics:** Sets, Relations, Complex Numbers, Matrices, Quadratic Equations, Sequences, Permutations, Binomial, Limits, Continuity, Differentiation, Integration, Differential Equations, Coordinate Geometry (2D + 3D), Vectors, Probability, Statistics, Trigonometry
**Physics:** Units, Kinematics, Laws of Motion, Work-Energy, Rotation, Gravitation, Properties of Matter, Thermodynamics, Oscillations, Waves, Electrostatics, Current Electricity, Magnetism, EMI, Optics, Modern Physics, Semiconductor
**Chemistry:** Physical (same as NEET), Organic (same as NEET + Polymers, Biomolecules), Inorganic (same as NEET)

### Key Characteristics
- PCM only (no Biology)
- Integer questions: no guessing possible, no negative marking
- Two sessions: students can improve score
- NTA Percentile scoring (not raw marks)
- Top ~2.5 lakh qualifiers attempt JEE Advanced

### Similar Exams (same/overlapping syllabus)
- BITSAT (same PCM syllabus + English + Logical Reasoning)
- VITEEE (PCM + Aptitude + English)
- MHTCET (Maharashtra, same PCM)
- KCET (Karnataka, same PCM)
- WBJEE (West Bengal, PCM)
- COMEDK (Karnataka, PCM)
- SRMJEEE, KIITEE (private universities, same PCM)

## Exam 3: JEE Advanced

**Conducting body:** IITs (rotating)
**Candidates:** 1.5–2 lakh (pre-filtered by JEE Main rank)
**Purpose:** Admission to IITs, ISM Dhanbad

### Pattern
Two papers, 3 hours each, same day.

**Paper 1 & 2 each contain (varies by year):**
| Section | Type | Marks | Negative |
|---|---|---|---|
| Single Correct | MCQ | +3/−1 | Yes |
| Multi Correct | MCQ (1-4 correct options) | +4 full / partial / −2 | Yes |
| Integer Type | Numerical (0–9) | +3/0 | No |
| Matching List | Table matching | Variable | Yes |
| **Total per paper** | | ~180 | |

**Total:** 360 marks across both papers

### Syllabus
Same as JEE Main but with deeper application requirements. Key additions:
- Paragraph-based questions (comprehension of a scenario)
- Experimental data interpretation
- Cross-chapter multi-step problems
- No fixed syllabus document — IITs set their own standard

### Key Characteristics
- Multi-correct: most cognitively demanding question type in any Indian exam
- Paragraph questions: 3–4 questions from one situation (context-dependent)
- Cross-chapter synthesis is mandatory, not occasional
- Most questions have no direct formula — require derivation or approximation
- See `JEE_ADVANCED_360_CHITTA.md` for full cognitive science analysis

## Exam 4: UPSC Civil Services Examination

**Conducting body:** UPSC
**Candidates:** 10–12 lakh (Prelims forms); ~5 lakh appear; ~900 final selections
**Purpose:** IAS, IPS, IFS, IRS and other Group A/B Central Services

### Pattern (3 stages)

**Stage 1: Prelims**
| Paper | Questions | Marks | Negative |
|---|---|---|---|
| GS Paper 1 | 100 MCQ | 200 | −0.66 |
| CSAT (Paper 2) | 80 MCQ | 200 | −0.66 |

CSAT is qualifying only (33% needed). GS Paper 1 is the filter.
Duration: 2 hours each.

**Stage 2: Mains (descriptive, 9 papers)**
| Paper | Marks | Nature |
|---|---|---|
| Essay | 250 | 2 essays |
| GS 1 (History, Geography, Society) | 250 | Descriptive |
| GS 2 (Governance, IR, Constitution) | 250 | Descriptive |
| GS 3 (Economy, Environment, Science & Tech) | 250 | Descriptive |
| GS 4 (Ethics, Integrity) | 250 | Descriptive |
| Optional Paper 1 | 250 | Chosen subject |
| Optional Paper 2 | 250 | Chosen subject |
| English (qualifying) | 300 | — |
| Indian Language (qualifying) | 300 | — |
| **Total counted** | **1750** | |

**Stage 3: Personality Test (Interview)**
275 marks

**Total: 2025 marks**

### GS Prelims Syllabus
- History: Ancient, Medieval, Modern India, Art & Culture, Post-independence
- Geography: Physical, Indian, World, Environment & Ecology
- Economy: Basics, Budget, Banking, Schemes, International bodies
- Polity: Constitution, Parliament, Judiciary, Federalism, Local bodies
- Science & Tech: Current developments, Space, Defence, Biotech
- Environment: Climate, Biodiversity, Pollution, Conventions
- Current Affairs: 12–18 months rolling window

### Key Characteristics
- Prelims: elimination round — only top 12–13x of vacancies clear
- Mains: writing quality + breadth of knowledge + analytical depth
- Optional subject: 500 marks can be a major differentiator
- Current affairs: dynamic syllabus, always changing
- Interview: personality, not knowledge — communication, presence, judgment

### Similar Exams (same GS syllabus)
- MPPSC (Madhya Pradesh PSC) — same GS structure, MP-specific additions
- UPPSC (Uttar Pradesh PSC) — same GS + UP-specific
- BPSC (Bihar PSC) — same GS + Bihar-specific
- HPSC, RPSC, KPSC (state PSCs) — same core GS framework
- UPSC CDS (Defence) — GS + Maths + English

## Exam 5: SSC CGL

**Conducting body:** Staff Selection Commission
**Candidates:** 15–20 lakh annually
**Purpose:** Group B and C posts: Income Tax, CBI, Auditor, Assistant, etc.

### Pattern (4 tiers)

**Tier 1 (Computer Based, screening)**
| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| General Intelligence & Reasoning | 25 | 50 | Combined |
| General Awareness | 25 | 50 | Combined |
| Quantitative Aptitude | 25 | 50 | Combined |
| English Comprehension | 25 | 50 | Combined |
| **Total** | **100** | **200** | **60 min** |
Negative: −0.5 per wrong

**Tier 2 (Computer Based, main)**
| Paper | Sections | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Paper 1 (All posts) | Math + Reasoning + English + GK | 390 | 2h 30m |
| Paper 2 (Stats posts) | Statistics | 200 | 2 hours |
| Paper 3 (Finance posts) | Finance & Economics | 200 | 2 hours |

### Syllabus
**Quantitative Aptitude:** Number system, Simplification, Percentage, Ratio, Average, Profit/Loss, SI/CI, Time-Speed-Distance, Pipes, Geometry, Mensuration, Trigonometry, Algebra, DI
**Reasoning:** Analogy, Classification, Series, Coding-Decoding, Blood Relations, Directions, Syllogism, Venn Diagrams, Matrix, Missing Numbers, Non-verbal
**English:** Reading Comprehension, Fill in the Blanks, Error Spotting, Cloze Test, Para Jumbles, Synonyms/Antonyms, Idioms, One Word Substitution
**GK/GA:** Static GK (History, Geography, Polity, Economy, Science), Current Affairs

### Key Characteristics
- Tier 1 is speed + accuracy — 200 marks in 60 minutes = 18 seconds per question
- No depth required — breadth and speed dominate
- Reasoning section is heavily learnable through pattern practice
- GK is static + current; current affairs from last 6 months

### Similar Exams (same core syllabus)
- SSC CHSL (10+2 level, same structure, slightly easier)
- SSC MTS (easier, no Tier 2)
- SSC CPO (Police — same + Physical)
- SSC Stenographer (same + typing test)
- IBPS PO, IBPS Clerk (banking variant of same core)
- SBI PO (banking variant, higher difficulty)
- RRB NTPC (Railway variant, same core)

## Exam 6: Railway Recruitment Board Exams

**Conducting body:** RRBs (21 regional boards)
**Candidates:** 1–2 crore (biggest exam in the world by applicants)
**Purpose:** Railway jobs across all levels

### Major RRB Exams

| Exam | Post | Candidates | Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| RRB NTPC | Station Master, Goods Guard, Clerk | 1–1.5 crore | Graduate |
| RRB Group D | Track Maintainer, Helper | 1–2 crore | 10th pass |
| RRB JE | Junior Engineer | 10–15 lakh | Diploma/Degree |
| RRB ALP | Assistant Loco Pilot | 30–40 lakh | ITI/10th |

### Pattern (RRB NTPC — most popular)
**CBT 1 (Screening):**
100 questions, 90 minutes: Mathematics (30) + GI & Reasoning (30) + General Awareness (40)
Negative: −1/3 per wrong

**CBT 2 (Main):**
120 questions, 90 minutes: Mathematics (35) + GI & Reasoning (35) + General Awareness (50)

### Syllabus
**Mathematics:** Number system, Decimals, Fractions, LCM/HCF, Ratio, Percentage, Mensuration, Time-Work, Time-Speed, SI/CI, Profit-Loss, Elementary Algebra, Geometry, Trigonometry
**Reasoning:** Analogies, Completion, Coding-Decoding, Mathematical Operations, Similarities, Relationships, Syllogism, Jumbling, Venn Diagrams, Conclusions
**General Awareness:** Science & Technology, Sports, Culture, Personalities, Economics, Polity, Environment, Railway-specific GK, Current Affairs

### Key Characteristics
- Largest absolute candidate pool in any exam worldwide
- Lower difficulty than SSC CGL but extremely competitive due to volume
- Railway-specific GK is a differentiator
- Speed is everything: 100 questions in 90 minutes

## Exam 7: IBPS PO

**Conducting body:** IBPS (Institute of Banking Personnel Selection)
**Candidates:** 5–8 lakh annually
**Purpose:** Probationary Officer in 11 public sector banks

### Pattern

**Prelims:**
| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| English Language | 30 | 30 | 20 min |
| Quantitative Aptitude | 35 | 35 | 20 min |
| Reasoning Ability | 35 | 35 | 20 min |
| **Total** | **100** | **100** | **60 min** |

**Mains:**
| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Reasoning & Computer | 45 | 60 | 60 min |
| English Language | 35 | 40 | 40 min |
| Data Analysis & Interpretation | 35 | 60 | 45 min |
| General/Economy/Banking Awareness | 40 | 40 | 35 min |
| **Total** | **155** | **200** | **3 hours** |

**Interview:** 100 marks
**Final:** Mains (80%) + Interview (20%)

### Syllabus
**Quantitative Aptitude / Data Interpretation:** Same as SSC CGL Quant + DI charts (Bar, Line, Pie, Table, Caselet), Data Sufficiency
**Reasoning:** Same as SSC + Seating Arrangement (complex), Puzzles, Input-Output, Blood Relations (complex), Coding-Decoding, Inequality, Statement-Conclusion
**English:** Reading Comprehension (dense passages), Cloze Test, Para Jumbles, Fill in the Blanks, Error Detection, Sentence Improvement, Word Usage
**Banking Awareness:** RBI functions, monetary policy, banking terms, financial awareness, schemes, recent banking news
**Computer:** Basic hardware/software, MS Office, Internet, Networking basics

### Key Characteristics
- Speed + accuracy in Quant and Reasoning is the filter
- Banking awareness differentiates candidates at mains stage
- Mains has sectional time limits — cannot compensate weak section
- Interview tests communication, banking knowledge, current affairs

### Similar Exams (same pattern/syllabus)
- **SBI PO** (harder — Mains has more complex puzzles + English)
- **IBPS Clerk** (no interview, simpler puzzles)
- **SBI Clerk** (no interview, moderate difficulty)
- **RBI Grade B** (much harder + Economics + Finance + Management)
- **SEBI Grade A** (Finance + Law + Securities knowledge)
- **NABARD Grade A** (Agriculture + Rural Development + Finance)
- **LIC AAO** (Insurance + Finance variant)
- **NIACL AO** (Insurance variant)

## Exam 8: SBI PO

**Conducting body:** SBI (State Bank of India)
**Candidates:** 8–10 lakh annually
**Purpose:** Probationary Officer in SBI

### Pattern
Same structure as IBPS PO but:
- Mains: includes a **Descriptive Paper** (1 hour, 50 marks) — Letter + Essay writing
- Reasoning puzzles are harder (Floor puzzles, Complex arrangements)
- English questions are more inference-heavy
- Interview is called GDPI (Group Discussion + Personal Interview)

### Key Characteristics
- SBI is the gold standard of banking jobs — higher competition density than IBPS
- Descriptive paper is unique — IBPS PO does not have it
- GD tests group communication, leadership, current affairs articulation

## Exam 9: CUET

**Conducting body:** NTA
**Candidates:** 10–14 lakh (rapidly growing since 2022)
**Purpose:** Central University undergraduate admissions (DU, BHU, JNU, etc.)

### Pattern
| Component | Details |
|---|---|
| Section IA | 13 Indian languages (student chooses 1–2) |
| Section IB | 20 foreign languages (optional) |
| Section II | 27 domain subjects (student chooses up to 6) |
| Section III | General Test (optional) |

**Domain subjects** (Section II): PCM, PCB, Commerce, Humanities, Agriculture, etc.
**General Test:** GK + Current Affairs + General Mental Ability + Quantitative Reasoning + Logical Reasoning + Language Comprehension

Each section: 45–50 questions, 60 minutes, MCQ, −1 per wrong

### Key Characteristics
- Completely NCERT-based (12th standard only)
- Domain subjects follow the exact 12th NCERT syllabus
- Can substitute for board marks for DU admissions
- Growing rapidly — may replace board marks entirely in future
- Logical Reasoning + Language sections overlap with SSC/Banking

### Similar Exams
- **IPU CET** (same domain structure, Delhi university colleges)
- **BHU UET** (similar NCERT-based structure)
- State-level CETs for undergraduate admissions

## Exam 11: GATE

**Conducting body:** IITs + IISc
**Candidates:** 5–8 lakh annually
**Purpose:** PSU jobs (ONGC, BHEL, NTPC etc.) + PG admission to IITs/NITs

### Pattern
| Section | Questions | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| General Aptitude | 10 (5 MCQ + 5 MCQ) | 15 |
| Core Engineering/Science | 55 (MCQ + MSQ + Numerical) | 85 |
| **Total** | **65** | **100** |

**Duration:** 3 hours
**Negative:** −1/3 for MCQ, 0 for MSQ and Numerical
**29 papers** (CE, CS, EE, ME, EC, CH, IN, etc.)

### Syllabus (varies by paper — CS example)
**CS/IT:** Engineering Mathematics (Linear Algebra, Calculus, Probability, Discrete Maths) + Algorithms + Data Structures + OS + DBMS + Computer Networks + Theory of Computation + Compiler Design + Digital Logic + Computer Organization

### Key Characteristics
- GATE score valid 3 years for PSU recruitment
- GATE CS is the toughest CS exam in India — requires both breadth and depth
- General Aptitude section is same across all 29 papers
- Numerical answer questions make it harder than JEE (no elimination)
- Percentile-based: GATE score 750+ (out of 1000) needed for top IITs

### Similar Exams
- **IES/ESE** (Engineering Services — harder, includes technical interview)
- **CSIR NET** (Science research — Biology, Chemistry, Physics, Maths, Earth Sciences)
- **UGC NET** (Teaching — similar structure, all humanities/science subjects)
- **BARC OCES/DGFS** (Nuclear research — very hard, GATE-level + interview)
- **ISRO Scientist** (Aerospace/CS/Electronics — GATE-level)

## Exam 12: CAT (Common Admission Test)

**Conducting body:** IIMs (rotating)
**Candidates:** 2–3 lakh annually
**Purpose:** MBA admissions to IIMs and 1200+ MBA colleges

### Pattern
| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Verbal Ability & RC (VARC) | 24 | 72 | 40 min |
| Data Interpretation & Logical Reasoning (DILR) | 20 | 60 | 40 min |
| Quantitative Ability (QA) | 22 | 66 | 40 min |
| **Total** | **66** | **198** | **2 hours** |

**Negative:** −1 per wrong MCQ; 0 for TITA (type-in-the-answer)
**Scaled score** → percentile

### Syllabus
**VARC:** Reading Comprehension (4–5 passages, dense), Para Jumbles, Para Summary, Odd Sentence Out, Inferences — all higher-order comprehension
**DILR:** Puzzles (seating, scheduling, routes), Tables, Graphs, Networks, Games & Tournaments, Caselets — no fixed formula-based DI
**QA:** Arithmetic (Percent, Ratio, TSD, Work), Algebra (Equations, Functions, Polynomials), Geometry (Circles, Triangles, Mensuration), Number Theory, Combinatorics, Probability, P&L

### Key Characteristics
- No static syllabus — CAT rewards reasoning ability, not rote preparation
- VARC is the differentiator at 99+ percentile — cannot be "covered" in 3 months
- DILR sets have no formula — pure on-the-spot problem solving
- Sectional time limits: cannot skip a weak section
- IIM call criteria: sectional + overall cutoffs simultaneously

### Similar Exams (CAT-family)
- **XAT** (harder DILR + Decision Making section — unique to XAT)
- **NMAT** (no negative marking, 3 attempts per year)
- **SNAP** (Symbiosis — simpler, faster)
- **IIFT** (Trade + Economics + GK heavy)
- **CMAT** (NTA-conducted, easier, for AICTE colleges)
- **MAT** (easiest MBA entrance, very wide college coverage)

## Exam 13: CLAT (Common Law Admission Test)

**Conducting body:** Consortium of NLUs
**Candidates:** 60k–1 lakh annually
**Purpose:** LLB admissions to 22 National Law Universities (NLUs)

### Pattern
| Section | Questions | Marks |
|---|---|---|
| English Language | 22–26 | 22–26 |
| Current Affairs & GK | 35–39 | 35–39 |
| Legal Reasoning | 35–39 | 35–39 |
| Logical Reasoning | 28–32 | 28–32 |
| Quantitative Techniques | 13–17 | 13–17 |
| **Total** | **120** | **120** |

**Duration:** 2 hours
**Negative:** −0.25 per wrong
**Passage-based:** All sections are passage/comprehension-based since 2020

### Syllabus
**English:** Reading comprehension passages — inference, tone, vocabulary in context
**Current Affairs:** Passage-based questions on recent news, legal events, historical events
**Legal Reasoning:** Principle + Fact format — apply given legal principle to a factual scenario (no prior legal knowledge needed)
**Logical Reasoning:** Argument analysis, inference drawing, strengthening/weakening arguments
**Quantitative Techniques:** 10th-standard Maths — ratios, percentages, basic statistics, data interpretation

### Key Characteristics
- No prior legal knowledge required — reasoning ability is the test
- Passage-based format rewards reading speed + inference
- Legal Reasoning is unique — tests logical application of rules
- English reading speed is the strongest predictor of CLAT rank
- NLSIU Bangalore (CLAT rank 1) is the target for most serious aspirants

### Similar Exams
- **AILET** (NLU Delhi — harder, separate exam, no passage format)
- **SLAT** (Symbiosis — easier)
- **LSAT India** (Pearson — US-style logical reasoning, no GK)
- **MH CET Law** (Maharashtra)
- **TS LAWCET / AP LAWCET** (state-level)

## Exam 15: ISI Admission Test

**Conducting body:** Indian Statistical Institute
**Candidates:** ~5,000–10,000 (most elite ratio in India — ~30 seats)
**Purpose:** B.Stat, B.Math, M.Stat, MS(QE) admission

### Pattern (B.Stat / B.Math)

**PSA (Objective):**
30 questions, 2 hours, MCQ, no negative marking

**PSB (Short Answer/Proof):**
10 problems, 2 hours, descriptive solutions required

### Syllabus
**B.Stat/B.Math:** Higher Algebra (Groups, Rings, Fields — beyond 12th), Real Analysis, Combinatorics, Number Theory, Coordinate Geometry, Calculus, Probability

### Key Characteristics
- Hardest entrance exam for mathematics in India — harder than JEE Advanced Maths
- PSB requires written proofs — no MCQ for the main paper
- ISI B.Stat is the direct path to Indian Statistical Service and research
- Questions test mathematical maturity, not speed
