## CLASS 11 PHYSICS

### Unit 1 — Physical World & Measurement

**Chapter 1: Physical World**
- Scope and excitement of physics
- Physics in relation to technology and society
- Fundamental forces in nature (gravitational, electromagnetic, strong nuclear, weak nuclear)
- Nature of physical laws

**Chapter 2: Units and Measurements**
- International System of Units (SI units)
- Base units and derived units
- Significant figures and rounding rules
- Dimensions of physical quantities
- Dimensional analysis and its applications
  - Checking dimensional consistency
  - Deriving relations between physical quantities
  - Converting units between systems
- Errors in measurement
  - Systematic errors and random errors
  - Absolute error, relative error, percentage error
  - Propagation of errors (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, powers)
- Parallax method for measuring large distances
- Estimation of very small distances (X-ray diffraction, electron microscope)

### Unit 2 — Kinematics

**Chapter 3: Motion in a Straight Line**
- Position, path length, displacement
- Average velocity and average speed
- Instantaneous velocity and speed
- Acceleration (average and instantaneous)
- Kinematic equations for uniformly accelerated motion
  - v = u + at
  - s = ut + ½at²
  - v² = u² + 2as
  - s_n = u + a(2n−1)/2
- Motion under gravity (free fall, vertical throw)
- Relative velocity in one dimension
- Position-time graph, velocity-time graph, acceleration-time graph interpretation

**Chapter 4: Motion in a Plane**
- Scalars and vectors
- Position vector and displacement vector
- Equality of vectors, multiplication by a scalar
- Addition and subtraction of vectors — graphical method (triangle law, parallelogram law)
- Resolution of vectors into components
- Unit vectors (î, ĵ, k̂)
- Dot product (scalar product) and its properties
- Cross product (vector product) and its properties
- Projectile motion
  - Horizontal projectile
  - Oblique projectile — time of flight, range, maximum height
  - Equation of trajectory
- Uniform circular motion
  - Angular velocity and angular acceleration
  - Centripetal acceleration
  - Relation v = rω

### Unit 3 — Laws of Motion

**Chapter 5: Laws of Motion**
- Aristotle's fallacy and Galileo's law of inertia
- Newton's first law — concept of force and inertia
- Newton's second law — F = ma, momentum, impulse
- Newton's third law — action-reaction pairs
- Conservation of momentum
- Equilibrium of a particle — concurrent forces
- Common forces in mechanics
  - Weight (mg)
  - Normal reaction
  - Tension in strings
  - Spring force (Hooke's Law)
  - Friction (static, kinetic, rolling)
- Laws of friction — μ_s and μ_k
- Circular motion dynamics — centripetal force
- Problems: Atwood machine, pulley systems, inclined planes
- Banking of roads (with and without friction)

### Unit 4 — Work, Energy and Power

**Chapter 6: Work, Energy and Power**
- Work done by a constant force — W = F·d·cosθ
- Work done by a variable force — integration, work-energy theorem
- Kinetic energy — KE = ½mv²
- Work-energy theorem
- Potential energy — gravitational PE, spring PE
- Conservative and non-conservative forces
- Conservation of mechanical energy
- Power — P = W/t = F·v
- Collisions
  - Elastic collision (1D and 2D)
  - Inelastic collision
  - Perfectly inelastic collision
  - Coefficient of restitution

### Unit 5 — Motion of System of Particles and Rigid Body

**Chapter 7: System of Particles and Rotational Motion**
- Centre of mass — definition, calculation for discrete and continuous systems
- Motion of centre of mass — Newton's second law for system
- Linear momentum of a system — conservation
- Angular velocity and angular acceleration
- Torque and angular momentum — τ = r × F, L = r × p
- Torque-angular momentum relation — τ = dL/dt
- Conservation of angular momentum
- Equilibrium of rigid bodies — translational + rotational
- Moment of inertia
  - Definition: I = Σmr²
  - Theorems: parallel axis theorem, perpendicular axis theorem
  - MI of standard bodies: ring, disc, solid sphere, hollow sphere, rod, cylinder
- Kinematics of rotational motion — θ, ω, α analogies
- Dynamics of rotational motion — τ = Iα
- Rolling motion (without slipping) — KE of rolling body

### Unit 6 — Gravitation

**Chapter 8: Gravitation**
- Kepler's laws of planetary motion
  - Law of orbits (elliptical orbits)
  - Law of areas (equal areas in equal times — conservation of angular momentum)
  - Law of periods (T² ∝ r³)
- Universal law of gravitation — F = Gm₁m₂/r²
- Gravitational constant G
- Acceleration due to gravity (g) — variation with height, depth, latitude, shape of earth
- Gravitational potential energy — U = −GMm/r
- Gravitational potential — V = −GM/r
- Escape speed — v_e = √(2gR)
- Orbital velocity of satellites — v_o = √(gR) for low orbit
- Geostationary satellites and polar satellites
- Weightlessness

### Unit 7 — Properties of Bulk Matter

**Chapter 9: Mechanical Properties of Solids**
- Elastic behaviour of solids
- Stress and strain — types (tensile, shear, bulk)
- Hooke's law and elastic limit
- Young's modulus, Shear modulus (Rigidity modulus), Bulk modulus
- Poisson's ratio
- Elastic potential energy stored in a stretched wire
- Applications: stress-strain curve, breaking stress, ductile vs brittle materials

**Chapter 10: Mechanical Properties of Fluids**
- Pressure — definition, Pascal's law, pressure variation with depth
- Buoyancy — Archimedes' principle, apparent weight
- Streamline and turbulent flow
- Equation of continuity — A₁v₁ = A₂v₂
- Bernoulli's theorem and its applications
  - Venturimeter
  - Magnus effect, lift on aerofoil
- Viscosity — Newton's law of viscosity, coefficient of viscosity
- Stokes' law — terminal velocity
- Reynolds number — laminar vs turbulent flow
- Surface tension — surface energy, angle of contact
  - Excess pressure inside drops and bubbles
  - Capillary rise formula

**Chapter 11: Thermal Properties of Matter**
- Temperature scales — Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin
- Thermal expansion — linear, area, volume expansion; coefficients α, β, γ; relation β = 2α, γ = 3α
- Anomalous expansion of water
- Specific heat capacity — Q = mcΔT
- Calorimetry — principle, heat transfer
- Change of state — latent heat, melting, boiling, sublimation
- Heat transfer
  - Conduction — Fourier's law, thermal conductivity, R-value
  - Convection
  - Radiation — blackbody radiation, Stefan's law, Wien's displacement law
- Newton's law of cooling

### Unit 8 — Thermodynamics

**Chapter 12: Thermodynamics**
- Thermodynamic state, equilibrium, quasi-static process
- Zeroth law of thermodynamics
- First law of thermodynamics — ΔU = Q − W
- Work done in thermodynamic processes
  - Isothermal, adiabatic, isochoric, isobaric
  - P-V diagrams
- Heat engine — efficiency η = 1 − Q₂/Q₁
- Refrigerator and heat pump — COP
- Second law of thermodynamics — Kelvin-Planck and Clausius statements
- Carnot engine — Carnot cycle, Carnot theorem, maximum efficiency
- Reversible and irreversible processes
- Entropy (conceptual)

### Unit 9 — Behaviour of Perfect Gas and Kinetic Theory

**Chapter 13: Kinetic Theory**
- Molecular nature of matter
- Behaviour of gases — ideal gas equation PV = nRT
- Kinetic theory of an ideal gas
  - Pressure of an ideal gas — derivation
  - rms speed, mean speed, most probable speed
  - v_rms = √(3RT/M)
- Law of equipartition of energy
- Degrees of freedom — monoatomic, diatomic, polyatomic
- Specific heat capacities of gases — Cp, Cv, γ = Cp/Cv
- Mean free path

### Unit 10 — Oscillations and Waves

**Chapter 14: Oscillations**
- Periodic motion, period, frequency, displacement
- Simple harmonic motion (SHM) — definition, conditions
  - Restoring force F = −kx
  - x = A sin(ωt + φ)
  - Velocity and acceleration in SHM
  - Energy in SHM — KE, PE, total energy, energy-displacement graph
- Simple pendulum — T = 2π√(L/g), derivations, conditions
- Spring-mass system — T = 2π√(m/k)
- Series and parallel combinations of springs
- Damped oscillations, forced oscillations, resonance
- Free oscillations and natural frequency

**Chapter 15: Waves**
- Wave motion — transverse and longitudinal waves
- Speed of a transverse wave on a stretched string — v = √(T/μ)
- Speed of sound in a medium — Newton's formula, Laplace correction
- Displacement equation — y = A sin(kx − ωt)
- Wavelength, frequency, wave speed relation v = fλ
- Principle of superposition of waves
- Reflection of waves
- Standing waves — nodes and antinodes
- Standing waves in strings — harmonics and overtones
- Standing waves in pipes — open and closed ends — harmonics
- Beats — beat frequency = |f₁ − f₂|
- Doppler effect — apparent frequency formula (source and observer moving)

### Unit 1 — Electrostatics

**Chapter 1: Electric Charges and Fields**
- Electric charge — properties (quantisation, conservation, additivity)
- Coulomb's law — F = kq₁q₂/r² (in vacuum and medium)
- Superposition principle
- Electric field — definition, field lines, properties
- Electric field due to:
  - Point charge
  - Dipole (on axial and equatorial points)
  - Ring of charge (on axis)
  - Infinite line charge
  - Infinite plane sheet
  - Two parallel sheets
- Electric dipole — dipole moment, torque in uniform field, PE in field
- Electric flux — Φ = E·A·cosθ
- Gauss's law and its applications
  - Spherical shell (inside and outside)
  - Infinite line charge
  - Infinite plane sheet

**Chapter 2: Electrostatic Potential and Capacitance**
- Electric potential — definition, V = kq/r
- Potential difference, relation E = −dV/dr
- Potential due to:
  - Point charge
  - Dipole (axial and equatorial)
  - System of charges
- Equipotential surfaces — properties
- Potential energy of a system of charges
- Conductors in electrostatic fields — properties, surface charge distribution
- Dielectrics — polar and non-polar, polarisation, dielectric constant
- Capacitance — definition C = Q/V
- Capacitance of parallel plate capacitor — with and without dielectric
- Capacitance of spherical capacitor, cylindrical capacitor
- Series and parallel combination of capacitors
- Energy stored in a capacitor — U = ½CV² = Q²/2C = QV/2
- Effect of dielectric on capacitor (connected/disconnected from battery)
- Van de Graaff generator

### Unit 2 — Current Electricity

**Chapter 3: Current Electricity**
- Electric current — I = dQ/dt
- Drift velocity — I = nAev_d
- Ohm's law — V = IR, microscopic form J = σE
- Resistance — factors, temperature dependence, R = R₀(1 + αΔT)
- Resistivity and conductivity
- Series and parallel combination of resistors
- EMF and internal resistance — V = E − Ir
- Kirchhoff's laws (KCL and KVL)
- Wheatstone bridge — balanced condition
- Metre bridge — working, unknown resistance
- Potentiometer — principle, applications
  - Comparing EMFs
  - Internal resistance measurement
- Electrical energy and power — P = VI = I²R = V²/R
- Electric cell — primary and secondary
- Carbon resistors, colour code

### Unit 3 — Magnetic Effects of Current and Magnetism

**Chapter 4: Moving Charges and Magnetism**
- Magnetic force on a moving charge — F = q(v × B)
- Motion in combined electric and magnetic fields — velocity selector, cyclotron
- Magnetic force on a current-carrying conductor — F = IL × B
- Torque on a current loop in magnetic field — τ = NIAB sinθ
- Moving coil galvanometer — working, sensitivity, conversion to ammeter/voltmeter
- Biot-Savart law — dB = μ₀/4π · (Idl × r̂)/r²
- Magnetic field due to:
  - Straight wire (finite and infinite)
  - Circular loop (at centre and on axis)
  - Solenoid
  - Toroid
- Ampere's circuital law and applications

**Chapter 5: Magnetism and Matter**
- Bar magnet as equivalent solenoid
- Magnetic field lines, properties
- Magnetic field on axial and equatorial positions of bar magnet
- Torque and PE of a magnetic dipole in uniform field
- Gauss's law in magnetism — no magnetic monopoles
- Earth's magnetism — elements (B_H, angle of dip, angle of declination)
- Magnetisation and magnetic intensity — M, H, B = μ₀(H + M)
- Magnetic susceptibility and permeability
- Classification of magnetic materials — diamagnetic, paramagnetic, ferromagnetic
- Hysteresis loop — retentivity, coercivity
- Curie's law, Curie temperature

### Unit 4 — Electromagnetic Induction and Alternating Currents

**Chapter 6: Electromagnetic Induction**
- Faraday's laws of electromagnetic induction
- Lenz's law — conservation of energy
- Motional EMF — ε = Blv
- Magnetic flux — Φ = B·A·cosθ
- Eddy currents — causes and applications
- Self-induction — L, ε = −L dI/dt, energy stored U = ½LI²
- Mutual induction — M, ε₂ = −M dI₁/dt
- Coefficient of coupling
- Transformer — working, efficiency, step-up/step-down

**Chapter 7: Alternating Current**
- AC generator — working, EMF equation ε = ε₀ sin(ωt)
- Peak value, RMS value, average value
- Phasors and phasor diagrams
- AC circuits
  - Pure resistor (R circuit)
  - Pure inductor (L circuit) — inductive reactance X_L = ωL
  - Pure capacitor (C circuit) — capacitive reactance X_C = 1/ωC
  - LR, CR, LC, LCR series circuits
- Impedance — Z = √(R² + (X_L − X_C)²)
- Phase angle — tan φ = (X_L − X_C)/R
- Power in AC circuits — P = V_rms I_rms cosφ, power factor
- Resonance in LCR circuit — f₀ = 1/(2π√LC)
- Q-factor (quality factor)
- LC oscillations
- Transformer — turns ratio, energy losses

### Unit 5 — Electromagnetic Waves

**Chapter 8: Electromagnetic Waves**
- Displacement current — Ampere-Maxwell law
- Maxwell's equations (conceptual)
- Electromagnetic waves — properties, transverse nature
- Speed of EM waves — c = 1/√(μ₀ε₀) = 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- Electromagnetic spectrum (radio, microwave, infrared, visible, UV, X-ray, gamma)
  - Wavelength ranges
  - Sources and detectors
  - Uses and properties of each
- Energy density and intensity of EM waves

### Unit 6 — Optics

**Chapter 9: Ray Optics and Optical Instruments**
- Reflection at curved surfaces
  - Mirror formula — 1/v + 1/u = 1/f
  - Magnification m = −v/u
  - Concave and convex mirrors — sign convention, ray diagrams
- Refraction at plane surfaces
  - Snell's law — n₁ sinθ₁ = n₂ sinθ₂
  - Total internal reflection — critical angle
  - Applications: optical fibre, mirage, brilliance of diamond
- Refraction at spherical surfaces — 1/v − 1/u = (n₂ − n₁)/(n₁R)
- Thin lens formula — 1/v − 1/u = 1/f
- Lens maker's equation — 1/f = (n − 1)(1/R₁ − 1/R₂)
- Power of a lens — P = 1/f
- Combination of lenses and mirrors
- Refraction through a prism
  - Prism formula: n = sin((A + D)/2) / sin(A/2)
  - Minimum deviation condition
- Dispersion — angular dispersion, dispersive power
- Scattering of light — Rayleigh scattering, blue sky, red sunset
- Optical instruments
  - Human eye — accommodation, near point, far point, defects (myopia, hypermetropia, presbyopia, astigmatism) and corrections
  - Simple microscope — magnifying power
  - Compound microscope — magnifying power
  - Astronomical telescope — magnifying power, resolving power
  - Reflecting telescope

**Chapter 10: Wave Optics**
- Wavefront — Huygens' principle
- Reflection and refraction using Huygens' principle
- Coherent sources — conditions
- Young's double slit experiment (YDSE)
  - Fringe width β = λD/d
  - Conditions for bright and dark fringes
  - Effect of introducing slabs, immersing in medium
- Interference — constructive and destructive
- Diffraction
  - Single slit diffraction — central maximum width = 2λD/a
  - Condition for minima: a sinθ = nλ
  - Difference between interference and diffraction
- Polarisation
  - Polarisation by reflection — Brewster's angle
  - Malus's law — I = I₀ cos²θ
  - Polaroids and applications
  - Optical activity

### Unit 7 — Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter

**Chapter 11: Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter**
- Photoelectric effect — Hertz and Lenard experiments
- Einstein's photoelectric equation — KE_max = hν − φ
- Work function, threshold frequency, stopping potential
- Photoelectric effect: effect of frequency and intensity
- Wave nature of particles — de Broglie hypothesis λ = h/mv = h/p
- Davisson-Germer experiment (confirmation of de Broglie)
- Heisenberg's uncertainty principle — ΔxΔp ≥ h/4π

### Unit 8 — Atoms and Nuclei

**Chapter 12: Atoms**
- Thomson's atomic model — limitations
- Rutherford's nuclear model — alpha scattering experiment, distance of closest approach, impact parameter
- Bohr's model of hydrogen atom
  - Postulates
  - Radius of orbits — r_n = n²a₀ (a₀ = 0.529 Å)
  - Energy of orbits — E_n = −13.6/n² eV
  - Velocity of electron
  - Hydrogen spectrum — Lyman, Balmer, Paschen, Brackett, Pfund series
- de Broglie explanation of Bohr's quantisation
- Limitations of Bohr's model
- X-rays — characteristic and continuous

**Chapter 13: Nuclei**
- Composition of nucleus — protons, neutrons, atomic number, mass number
- Nuclear size — R = R₀A^(1/3)
- Mass defect and binding energy — BE = Δm × c²
- Binding energy per nucleon — BE/A vs A curve
- Nuclear forces — properties
- Radioactivity — alpha, beta, gamma decay
  - Laws of radioactive decay — N = N₀e^(−λt)
  - Half-life T₁/₂ = 0.693/λ
  - Mean life τ = 1/λ
  - Activity A = λN
- Alpha decay — mass number −4, atomic number −2
- Beta decay — β⁻, β⁺, electron capture
- Gamma decay — no change in A or Z
- Nuclear fission — chain reaction, critical mass
- Nuclear fusion — stellar energy, thermonuclear reaction
- Nuclear reactor — components and working

### Unit 9 — Electronic Devices

**Chapter 14: Semiconductor Electronics**
- Energy bands in solids — conductor, semiconductor, insulator
- Intrinsic semiconductor — electron-hole pairs, ni
- Extrinsic semiconductor
  - n-type (donor impurity — pentavalent)
  - p-type (acceptor impurity — trivalent)
- p-n junction — formation, depletion region, barrier potential
- p-n junction diode
  - Forward bias and reverse bias
  - V-I characteristics
  - Dynamic resistance
  - Diode as half-wave rectifier
  - Full-wave rectifier (centre-tap and bridge)
  - Filters (basic concept)
- Special purpose diodes
  - Zener diode — Zener breakdown, voltage regulation
  - LED — principle, uses
  - Photodiode — working, applications
  - Solar cell — working, I-V characteristics
- Junction transistor (BJT)
  - NPN and PNP structure
  - Active, cutoff, saturation regions
  - CB, CE, CC configurations
  - CE characteristics (input and output)
  - Transistor as amplifier (CE) — voltage gain, current gain
  - Transistor as switch
- Digital electronics
  - Logic gates — AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR
  - Truth tables
  - Boolean algebra (basic)
  - Universal gates (NAND, NOR)
  - De Morgan's theorems
- Integrated circuits (brief)
